Small Form-Factor and Friendly Prototyping and Learning Platform

A compact, user-friendly platform for our daily prototyping and testing new stuff, ideal for robotics enthusiasts and learners. Its small form factor simplifies experimentation, making it accessible for a wide range of educational and development purposes. This compact, user-friendly tracked mobile vehicle is specifically designed for makers and beginners entering the world of robotics. It offers an accessible, plug-and-play system that’s ready for immediate use, simplifying the initial steps in robotic exploration and experimentation.

This tracked robot platform is designed for fast prototyping and frequent system testing. Its accessible hardware layout allows for easy modification, debugging, and rapid iteration. The simple control interface makes it ideal for learning, testing motor and sensor configurations, and experimenting with real-time commands. Ideal for engineers, makers, and beginners, it serves as an adaptable, everyday tool for robotics development and testing.

The small tracked robot platform is specifically for hardware testing in laboratory settings. Its modular design allows for quick swapping of components, enabling engineers and researchers to evaluate various sensors, motors, and control systems with minimal downtime. The open architecture facilitates easy access to circuitry and interfaces, promoting efficient troubleshooting and optimization during experiments. By providing a hands-on testing environment, this platform supports real-time data collection and analysis, making it an invaluable resource for validating hardware performance and reliability in robotics applications.

Dimensions Length: 340 mm x Width: 200 mm

The Geo-Lab, after extensive hardware testing and integration, resembles a chaotic workshop filled with exposed electronics and wires sprawling in all directions. However, this hands-on prototyping environment is crucial for rapid development and hardware iteration. The visible disarray reflects the iterative nature of engineering, where experimentation and adaptation lead to innovative solutions. Each disconnected wire and open circuit is a testament to the continuous learning process that fuels creativity and technological advancement in robotics. This approach not only accelerates the testing phase but also fosters an experimental mindset essential for refining and enhancing hardware designs.

The Geo-Lab's microcontroller unit (MCU) can be programmed via an Arduino-compatible programming interface. To achieve this, reflash an Arduino board as an ISP (In-System Programming) programmer. Once configured, the Arduino can serve as a bridge to program Geo-Lab’s MCU directly, streamlining the process without additional hardware. This setup supports custom firmware uploads and enables efficient prototyping on the Geo-Lab platform.

This setup, while appearing messy, represents an early prototype of the current RoboDrive Engine Control Board. Through extensive field testing and iterative adjustments, we refined the system to its current reliable form, now embedded in our Kenji-X1 and Torch Inspection AGVs. The onboard vibration scanner of the GEO-Lab has undergone extensive field testing, primarily indoors. Multiple testing cycles were conducted, and the data is represented in a detailed plot, demonstrating the scanner's high sensitivity and precision.

Link 1 LOX / RP-1
Link 2 981 kN / 220,500 lbf

The scanner successfully tracks the noise floor and captures discrete events, such as individual keystrokes, accurately recording each press and its timing relative to the sequence's start and between each keystroke. This data, presented by F.A.R.M. Potsdam laboratory, reflects real-world conditions and showcases the GEO-Lab's measurement capabilities.

The vibration scanner data from the GEO-Lab can be used in various practical and research applications. Each of these applications can leverage the real-time and precise nature of GEO-Lab's vibration data, enabling effective monitoring and proactive response in various settings.

Drivetrain

Tracked Drivetrain without suspension, small DC-Motors with Integrated Gearboxes.

Mast

The Mast-Cam offers full rotation of +/-180° horizontally (clockwise and counterclockwise) along with vertical up/down movement. This allows comprehensive, multi-angle coverage.

Payload

An optional payload capacity of up to 0.5 kg is available for transporting compact components, such as electronic modules or small-form-factor PCBs. This allows for flexible onboard integration of additional sensors or processing units.

Retrackable Arm

An optional retractable probe with an integrated vibration scanner is available, designed specifically for mounting on soft surfaces to capture precise vibration data. This setup enhances monitoring capabilities by providing adaptable surface contact for accurate readings in variable environments.
